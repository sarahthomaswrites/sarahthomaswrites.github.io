About Me

Sarah Thomas is a professional writer who writes within the fields of poetry and life-writing. She is passionate about blending the freedom and flexibility that creative writing permits the writer to create poems centred around themes from her real-life experiences. It is in this vein that she writes Polaris, a collection of short poems that highlight the feelings of achievement and other rewarding feelings associated with success, to inspire others to work on themselves. The theme of self-progression in Polaris reflects Sarah’s interest in exploring the untapped potential within ourselves and is indicative of where she finds subject matter inspiration to begin her writing process. She is currently conceptualizing Salt in the Sugar Bowl, a memoir detailing her immigration experience from Bahrain to Canada, and the bittersweet revelations she has made along the way.

As a content creator, she moves away from her introspective approach to creative writing. Instead, she values creating content that revolves around the highlights of her life experiences such as travelling. She faithfully travels with her best friend, Bridgette, and the pair capture content for their Instagram pages. The two are low-maintenance, budget-conscious travellers who speak to young adults who want to travel and need help learning how to maximize their available means. In a different light, Sarah is also exploring creating marketing and advertising content for upcoming business ventures she has in mind. She aims to use her background in writing to flesh out brand stories and customer engagement strategies.

Works

Creative works

Polaris

Breaking through familiar poetic themes of love and healing is Polaris, the book of poetry centred on inspiring young adults to be the North Star in their own sky. This collection calls for the romanticization of one’s potential and the immense feelings of pleasure and victory that follow as that potential is realized in coming to fruition. Polaris speaks directly to the drive and ambition teeming within young adults and works as a catalyst to launch readers into the reality of their greatest dreams.

Roasting Marshmallows

Three friends reunite around a fire pit roasting marshmallows on a late night in August. The pandemic has taken their last summer of freedom from them and University is starting soon. This ephemeron uncovers the fears and realizations that follow the young women on the cusp of adulthood as they slowly begin to see themselves as the roasting marshmallows.

Tracing Cross-Cultural Representations of Professionalism

Delving into scholarly perspectives on the historical rootings of professionalism, "Tracing Cross-Cultural Representations of Professionalism," examines Indian women's traditional business attire and the Western world's view of it alongside an experiential narrative. The piece serves to demonstrate how Western ideas of professionalism and professional attire are deeply rooted in White supremacy. It then recognizes the need for efforts that decenter the whiteness that is perpetuated through the standards of professionalism.

scholarly contributions

Escapism through Fantasy Themes of Cottagecore and Anime in Gucci’s “Flora Fantasy” Campaign

This rhetorical criticism paper analyzes Gucci’s “Flora Fantasy” campaign according to the fantasy-theme rhetorical criticism method that was developed by Ernest G. Bormann. The method serves as means of examining a shared worldview within a group, which within this campaign, reveals a promotion of fantastical escapism during a time that the world needed it most: the COVID-19 pandemic.

The Shackles of Mr. Grierson: An Analysis of “A Rose for Emily”

This literary analysis of the Southern gothic short story "A Rose for Emily" by William Faulkner examines the secretive life of Miss Emily Grierson, a mentally unstable spinster, and the everlasting power her deceased father has over her life. In an act of defiance against her father, Miss Emily murders her lover so that he may remain with her forever and give her the love she craved but never received. Through committing this heinous act, she ensures freedom from her father’s unloving grasp by securing control over her life in the areas she was previously submissive.
